An automated road will promote the efficiency of the whole town.

Human drivers often drive inefficiently by doing things like harshly accelerating or braking and idling.

A study finds that self-driving cars can be up to 15% more efficient just through better driving practices.

In the future, with completely autonomous roads, we can expect fleets and networks of these vehicles.

These networks can be managed on a macro scale, beyond just the town of Montgomery.

This opens up opportunities for even more optimization, including more efficient traffic flow, platooning of vehicles, and even new services like ride-sharing.

With your commitment and participation, we can further explore the possibilities in which we can interact with autonomous roads, and the ways in which this new technology can interact with us.

If a self-driving car is involved in an accident, who is liable?

In cases where the software is found to be at fault, manufacturers have agreed to assume full legal liability. If the reason for the crash was misuse of the software by a human operator, the operator of the vehicle is still liable.

Fully automated vehicles share information when communicating with other vehicles. Is my privacy protected?

All data shared with third parties is de-identified, meaning that no information that could be used to link to a specific vehicle or user is shared.

How can I trust the AI to not become unstable and take a mind of its own?

The AI of the cars has been meticulously researched and developed to be as robust as possible. In addition, we have a team of cybersecurity experts searching for anomalies in data.

When will I be able to use a self driving car in Montgomery?

The progress of these tests will be publically available, and you will be notified when a model becomes approved for public use.
